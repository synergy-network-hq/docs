1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help us remember your preferences, analyze usage, and provide a more personalized user experience.

2. Types of Cookies We Use
2.1 Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function. They enable core features such as account login, session management, privacy settings, and security compliance. Necessary cookies cannot be disabled.

2.2 Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our site. They collect information about page visits, user behavior, and website performance in an anonymized format. Examples include Plausible and Hotjar.

2.3 Preferences Cookies

Preferences cookies store your choices, such as language, display preferences, and other customizations. They allow the website to remember your settings and enhance your experience.

2.4 Marketing Cookies

Marketing cookies track your browsing habits to deliver content and advertisements tailored to your interests. Examples include AdRoll and other third-party ad services.

3. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

Service Delivery: Enable core website functionality.

Performance and Analytics: Monitor usage patterns, optimize website performance, and improve user experience.

Marketing and Personalization: Provide targeted content, offers, and promotions.

Compliance: Ensure compliance with legal requirements and security standards.

4. Your Choices

You may manage your cookie preferences at any time through our cookie banner or settings modal. Options include:

Accepting all cookies

Rejecting all non-essential cookies

Selecting specific cookie categories to allow or disallow

Disabling cookies may affect certain features or the website’s functionality.

5. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We may share anonymized analytics and marketing data with third-party providers such as Plausible, Hotjar, and AdRoll. These providers adhere to strict privacy standards and cannot use data beyond the purpose specified.
